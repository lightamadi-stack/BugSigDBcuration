# Gut Microbiota Composition in Pregnant Women with Preeclampsia

## Study Overview
This study investigated the gut microbiota composition of pregnant women with preeclampsia (PRE), including mild (P) and severe (SP) cases, compared to healthy controls (NOR).

## Study Design
- Design: Prospective case–control study  
- Matching: Cases and controls matched by age, gestational age, and BMI  
- Comparison groups:
  - PRE vs NOR  
  - SP vs P  

## Statistical Analyses
- Wilcoxon rank-sum test (Figure 7)  
- Mann–Whitney test (Figure 8)  
- LEfSe biomarker analysis (separate experiment)  
- Significance threshold: p < 0.05  

## Data Sources
- Figures: 5, 6, 7, 8  
- Table: 4  

## Alpha Diversity
- Simpson index: decreased in SP vs P (indicating increased diversity)
- Shannon index: unchanged  
- Chao1 index: unchanged  

## Differentially Abundant Taxa

### Increased in exposed group: Pregnant Women with Preeclampsia (PRE)
- *Megamonas*, *Synergistota*

### Decreased in exposed group: Pregnant Women with Preeclampsia (PRE)
- *Alistipes*, *Comamonadaceae*, *Comamonas*, *Deltaproteobacteria*, *Desulfovibrionaceae*, *Desulfovibrionales*, *Faecalibacterium*, *Oscillospiraceae*, *Rikenellaceae*, *Synergistaceae*, *Synergistales*, *Synergistia*, *Synergistota*, *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*, *[Clostridium] methylpentosum*

### Increased in exposed group: Pregnant Women with Severe Preeclampsia (SP)
- *Bacteria*, *Clostridium*, *Oscillospiraceae*, *Veillonella*, *Bacillota*, *Bacteroides uniformis*, *Enterocloster clostridioformis*, *Parabacteroides gordonii*

### Decreased in exposed group: Pregnant Women with Severe Preeclampsia (SP)
- *Phascolarctobacterium*, *Bacteroidota*, *Phocaeicola plebeius*		

## Curation Notes
